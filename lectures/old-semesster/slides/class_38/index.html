Nordhaus’s Perspective

Innovation

Source: Paul Maycock, Bloomberg New Energy Finance

  • Price has dropped from $80/Watt in 1976 to $0.22/Watt in 2019.
  • Dropped 99.7% (a factor of 360) in 43 years

Innovation Policy

  • Knowing price of CO2 will rise provides incentive to invest in R&D
  • Valley of Death:
    • Technology looks promising in laboratory
    • Potential for big profits
    • Many years, lots of money to turn laboratory device into product
      • Product development might fail
      • Product might not sell
      • Competitors might copy product
      • Valley of death
    • Double externality
      1. Inventors don’t get all the value of their inventions
      2. Polluters don’t pay the costs of pollution
    • Government support to cross valley of death

Image: Osawa & Miyazaki, Asian Journal of Technology Innovation 14, 93 (2006) doi: 10.1080/19761597.2006.9668620

Pielke’s Policy Proposal

Pielke’s Policy Proposal:

  • Competition within government
  • Public-works model
  • Demonstration projects
  • Government as consumer of energy innovations
    • Federal government is the largest consumer of energy in the U.S.
      • 350,000 buildings
      • 600,000 vehicles
  • $5/ton carbon tax ($0.04 per gallon gas)
    • invest in clean-energy R&D
  • Monitor progress
  • Develop “plan B” (geoengineering)
